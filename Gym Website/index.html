The Muscle Bar

Physical fitness is a general state of health and well-being and, more specifically, the ability to perform aspects of sports, occupations and daily activities. Physical fitness is generally achieved through proper nutrition, moderate-vigorous physical exercise, physical activity, and sufficient rest.

Services

We provide experienced, certified (personal) trainers and staff; the use of treadmills, rowing machines, cycling stations, and other cardiovascular equipment, the use of free weights such as barbells, dumbbells, and weight machines, swimming pool, maybe saunas, steam rooms, basketball courts, tanning, locker rooms, showers, and childcare services.

Cardio

Cardio machines like: treadmills, ellipticals, stationary bikes and stair steppers. Our gym also has rowing machines and spin bikes, which can help you improve your fitness of cardiovascular.

Resistance machines

Just like cardio machines, resistance machines have instructions printed on the side to teach you how to use them and set them up. You can also ask staff members at the gym for help if you get stuck.

Free weights

Compared to resistance machines like leg presses and Smith machines, free weights activate more muscles, burn extra calories, and increase your balance and flexibility more.

Functional training area

Functional exercises work multiple muscles and improve your fitness levels in a way that makes everyday activities like playing sports and picking up heavy bags of groceries easier.

Locker room

Personal training

Getting a personal trainer may help you reach your goals faster than you could on your own. Personal trainers can also help tailor your fitness routine to your goals and make sure you’re performing.

Studio for classes

Sporting courts (racquetball and basketball)

Many gyms have racquetball or basketball courts to allow you to burn calories while having fun with your buddies. Some even have leagues or private lessons to show you the ropes of the sport. Life Time Fitness, for example, has top of the line racquetball courts you can use anytime. They also have leagues, private and group lessons, and social play nights to allow you to meet new friends while working up a sweat. If racquetball isn’t your speed, you can also find basketball courts at some gyms like Equinox, Life Time, Gold’s Gym, YMCA, and 24 Hour Fitness.
